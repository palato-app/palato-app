## Structured notes (Jesse's write-up)

### Participant Coffee Profile

- Brews V60 every morning, 40g coffee at a 1:17 ratio for two people
- **Top buying criteria:** price-to-value, roast date, origin, and tasting notes (in that order)
- Target price range: $20–$22 for 12 oz — anything above $24 is a dealbreaker
- Buys ~3 bags/month from at least 2 roasters; spends ~$66/month on whole bean
- Favored roasters: Big Mouth Coffee Roasters, Bird and Branch (NYC)
- Rates ~15% of coffees as "incredible" and ~15% as "awful," with the majority landing in between
- Uses their phone's Photos app as a memory bank for coffees they enjoy
- Would not spend time tracking down a coffee they loved — moves on to the next best option
- Gifted or hard-to-reacquire coffees are not worth rating or logging
- Core motivation: maximize value — not seeking the highest quality at any price, but the best quality per dollar long-term

### Onboarding Feedback

- Described the onboarding as "painless" and "easy"
- Logo readability issue: the L and A were read as forming a "U," making it read as "Po-too"
- Skimmed over factoid/educational callouts during onboarding — not engaging enough to read
- Starting palate result felt "fine" and "not wrong," but did not deliver meaningful or motivating insight

### Rating & Logging Flow Feedback

- Flow was generally understood — user navigated to "Rate Coffee" from the catalog without prompting
- Would only log coffees they were genuinely impressed with; bad or one-off coffees don't feel worth the effort
- Felt significant fatigue midway through the form: *"Man, this is a lot of info for a coffee I'm never going to have again"*
- Confusion over whether the form is capturing one brew session or the overall bag experience
- Skipped the "add more details" section due to time pressure (child at home) — reflects real-world constraints

### Learn Section Feedback

- Found the section overwhelming — too much information presented all at once
- Wants toggle/collapsible dropdowns by region (e.g., click "Caribbean" to expand countries) rather than a flat wall of buttons
- Globe interaction is frustrating — expects to be able to spin and zoom freely; current limitations feel broken
- Page loading to the wrong scroll position when clicking a region compounds the frustration

### Palate & Discovery Feedback

- Palate development (tracking taste evolution over time) is the feature the user is *least* interested in
- Would engage with brew recommendations only if they include a brief explanation of *why* (e.g., "these tasting notes pop at this spec") and are backed by other users' experiences
- "You Might Like" recommendations should stay general (e.g., suggest a region or style), not hyper-specific bags that are hard to find locally
- Recommendations linked directly to a purchasable product page would drive click-through, even for someone who typically shops in person

### Overall App Impressions

- Described the app as: a coffee log/diary + educational tool + palate tracker — but **not** social and **not** e-commerce
- Would only open the app when genuinely excited about a coffee — not as a regular habit
- Sees high potential if the app consolidates: community ratings/tasting notes, browsing, and purchasing into one place
- Key quote: *"If it was going to recommend specific coffees, it needs to be like 2D coffee... I don't want another information silo"*
- Would not use the app if it required rating every coffee consumed
- Would recommend the app to a coffee-obsessed sibling and a curious newcomer to specialty coffee

### Strategic Direction Discussed

- Three potential directions identified: (1) data-rich personal coffee tracker ("Whoop for coffee"), (2) social platform for coffee drinkers, (3) palate-expansion + discovery tool
- E-commerce integration seen as critical for long-term value — recommendations must link only to available, accurately priced inventory
- Urban/brick-and-mortar use case is currently underserved — local shop recommendations would resonate strongly with NYC-based users
- Session ran 46 minutes — noted as needing to be tightened for future interviews

## Small / anecdotal observations (captured live, worth keeping)

- **Trigger for buying:** the last bag he bought hit all four criteria at once — roasted within a week, South American, ~$20–22, and tasting notes with "some character" (melon/peach) rather than the default "dark chocolate, nuts — everybody has that."
- **Value workaround:** splits larger coffee quantities with a buddy/coworker/brother to hit the value principle — "totally dependent on who's around when it's time to buy again."
- **Loss behavior:** the disappointing Sauté bag — "It's trash… and I'm gonna drink it tomorrow morning." Never throws coffee away; echoes Round 1 Theme 2.
- **Cold brew: hard no.** "Cold brew is fridge space… cold brew is time… I've [made] it for five years and I've tasted so much gross cold brew, I'm done."
- **Price fatalism:** believes coffee "is going to become a classism beverage" within 5–10 years; explicitly shopping value now "before it's done."
- **The gifted-bag logic chain (important for rating-supply assumptions):** didn't choose the coffee → can't easily re-buy it in his city → therefore not worth rating. Not about quality — about *actionability* of the memory.
- **9:19pm dead-end:** finished onboarding at night with no coffee to rate — "what do we do now?" Nothing in the app gives a next action when you can't brew.
- **MASL range behavior:** faced with "1200–1650 masl" and a single-number field, he didn't average — he backspaced and typed 1200. (Live confirmation of the known elevation-as-int debt.)
- **Descriptor matcher miss:** typed "chocolate covered almonds" — almond matched, chocolate didn't.
- **Rate field mis-tap:** trying to set the overall rating, he missed the dial and the page jumped to Body; also fought the body/acidity slider in mobile Chrome ("it doesn't seem to be sliding all that well").
- **Scale ambiguity:** had to ask "five is great, one is terrible?" before rating.
- **Required-fields confusion:** hit a validation stop on roast level without knowing which fields were required.
- **Edit-details surprise:** "Oh, I can edit details for a coffee I haven't tried? That feels weird." (Jesse, live: "should not be allowed.")
- **Lost recommendation:** navigated away from a "Try Something Unique" card and couldn't find his way back to it — "I'd like to go back to that recommendation."
- **No buy path from search:** searched Colombia in the catalog, picked a coffee, "I don't see a button to shop or buy or anything. So, I feel stuck." (Coffee wasn't augmented → no commerce block.)
- **Globe frustration verbatims:** "I can't spin it, like, forever. It's really lame." / "If it's a globe, it has to spin… It's not a globe if it doesn't spin." / tried to pinch-zoom and couldn't: "I'm so mad!"
- **Globe placement:** "A globe makes much more sense in the Learn section than it does the Palate section. My mouth is not a globe."
- **Trust threshold for recs:** "If Palato is going to so much work to describe me back to myself… I think I have earned my trust… and I'll trust those recommendations."
- **Price accuracy bar:** "If the price is going to be on the app, I would need it to be actually what the price is on the website too." And: "Don't lead me to a 404 or a sold-out coffee."
- **Shipping info:** explicitly *not* expected — "That you're leading me to a place where I can go purchase is kind of enough."
- **Timor-Leste moment:** wandered into Learn data he had no context for ("I don't know what this is, but I'm glad it's emerging") — curiosity, but also a sign the depth arrives before the framing.
