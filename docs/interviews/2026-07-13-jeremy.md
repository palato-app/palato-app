### App Walkthrough Feedback

**Onboarding Quiz**

- Felt conversational and engaging; "fun factoids" were a nice touch
- Origin selection should support multi-select (currently single-select only)
- Name pronunciation: "Palato" felt most natural to Jeremy
- Long URL on the sign-up/save screen was visually off-putting (joked about "saving to a Russian farm") — Jesse noted he needs to pay for a proper domain

**Rating Flow**

- Core fields (flavor notes, body, acidity, overall score) felt appropriate and not excessive — comparable to Q grading basics
- **Issue:** Body/acidity sliders lack numerical labels, making precise comparison across bags difficult
- **Issue:** Extraction, brew method, and dosing fields feel like too much for a first-entry experience; suggestion: make them a collapsible "advanced" section or a separate per-cup journal entry (like logging a specific brew session under a bag)
- Suggested keeping all fields editable after the fact so users can update notes over time
- General free-text notes field would be valuable for AI to help develop taste profiles

**Globe / Origin Map**

- Strong positive reaction overall: "no notes, this is sick"
- Harvest and elevation data highlights were particularly appreciated
- **Issue:** Globe scroll/spin sensitivity is too high on mobile — barely moving a finger causes rapid rotation
- Varietal names will go over most users' heads but are fine as reference (similar to skimming Wikipedia)
- **Feature request:** Show elevation data at the regional level within a country, not just country-level — this would help users predict flavor profile (higher elevation = sweeter, more complex)

### Future Features & Vision

- **Journal/memory feature** is what Jeremy would personally be most drawn to — logging specific brew sessions by date under each bag
- **Social layer (longer term):** Seeing what coffee influencers (e.g. Lance Hedrick) are brewing day-to-day would drive engagement
- **Profile "favorites" showcase:** Ability to pin top coffees on a public profile, similar to Letterboxd's top 4 films
- **Gamification:** Checking off coffees by country/region; motivates exploration and could drive purchasing behavior
- **Recommendation filters:** Users want both "community top-rated" and "aligned with my profile" options
- **Espresso:** Jesse plans to nail filter first; espresso would be a major future product drop — Jeremy agreed the flavor note tracking is harder for espresso but would value it for brew-parameter journaling (dose in/out, taste)
- **Monetization ideas discussed:** Affiliate model (commission per bag purchased through app) vs. subscription with free trial; promoted roaster placements as a potential revenue stream
