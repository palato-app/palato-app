### Meeting Context

- User interview and live app walkthrough for **Palato** ([palato.coffee](http://palato.coffee)), a specialty-coffee tracking and palate-building app built by Jesse Eshleman
- Interviewee is Joy, a specialty-coffee enthusiast of ~10 years
- **First session conducted without Jesse present** — the facilitator narrates Joy's on-screen actions where the recording wouldn't otherwise capture them

### Joy's Coffee Profile (Pre-App Interview)

- Drinking specialty coffee ~10 years; introduced by a family member (Jono) who worked at Intelligentsia
- Gateway experience: Bridgeport Coffee in Chicago; previously drank Dunkin' Donuts
- **Primary brew method:** pour over (V60 / Hario Switch), 1:16 ratio, 205°F water; 40g dose to 640g
- **Subscription:** Passenger (3 bags/month, ~$65–70) supplemented from Big Mouth (1–2 bags/month, ~$18–27); ~$100–$115/month for two people
- **Palate:** shifting from dark toward medium roast
- **Preferences:** slightly chocolatey/nutty; finds some coffees too acidic/"bitey"
- **Quality split:** ~10% outstanding / ~80% good / ~10% bad (a bell curve)
- **Constraint:** her burr grinder is malfunctioning (a part on back order), so she's hesitant to buy premium beans until it's fixed — doesn't want to waste good coffee on a "subpar" grind

### Key Pain Points (Pre-App)

- **No system to track liked/disliked coffees** — current method is photographing the label, which gets lost in the camera roll (or she just memorizes it)
- **Memorable coffees she can't re-purchase:** Big Shoulders' Ugandan Bugisu (seasonal/discontinued) and Onyx's Chalbesa Anaerobic (short seasonal window) — still thinks about both
- **Flavor notes on bags confuse her** — doesn't always taste what's described; unsure whether it's her palate, her brewing, or the roaster
- **Bought the same disliked coffee more than once** before realizing it (a "numbing" coffee at Trex)
- **Wants palate-based recommendations** — like the way Passenger asks about preferred notes
- **Rarely throws coffee out** — tolerates a bad bag (may ice it) rather than waste money

### App Walkthrough — Positive Reactions

- Immediately understood the app's purpose: "a customized personal library of coffees I like"
- Excited about palate-building: "as I add to my library it's going to expand and show what I really lean towards" — compared it to **"having your colors done"**
- Bag scanning well received — liked that the coffee was pre-filled from a shared catalog (compared it to wine-label scanning apps)
- Appreciated the buy link on catalog coffee pages
- Liked the country flags and detailed origin info (species, varieties, altitude) in Learn
- Found the origin map engaging — noticed the latitude band where coffee grows (tied it to the "10/40 window" from faith circles)
- Confirmed she'd use it and would add it to her home screen
- Would tell her pastor, Jay Lee (a dedicated coffee enthusiast who travels with a portable kit), about the app

### App Walkthrough — UX Feedback

- **Onboarding CTA** ("Unlock Your Palate") didn't signal account creation
- **"Rate Your First Coffee"** went to the catalog, not the rating screen — unexpected
- **Flavor-notes list** felt overwhelming as a large scrollable list; typing/searching felt more natural
- **Globe (Learn):** country outlines too subtle/dark — suggested dashed or faint continent outlines; spins too fast
- **"MASL"** unexplained — wants a tooltip / inline definition
- **"Somewhere else"** as the origin-affinity label on the palate page felt like an incomplete answer
- **Advanced brew details** (extraction, etc.) need inline education for less-experienced users
- **Supabase URL** exposed on the Google OAuth consent screen ("Rolfo Sousa was in the Supabase… it's crazy")

### Feature Requests & Suggestions

- **Brew-method education in Learn:** Kalita vs. V60, ratio guidance (not always 1:16)
- **Expert-recommended brew parameters per coffee** (ratio, temperature) to remove guesswork — "takes the guesswork out and I'm not wasting coffee"
- **Log a café coffee, not just a bag bought** — scan/ask-the-barista for the bag at a shop, or enter brew details manually, so a great in-shop cup gets recorded
- **Tone:** approachable and direct, not pretentious — referenced a condescending roaster experience (a snarky answer about wetting the filter)

### App Name Discussion

- Joy asked about the name — thought of gelato, and noted that in Italian, one "t" vs. two "t"s changes the sound
- Clarified the distinction: palate (taste / roof of the mouth) vs. palette (artist's board) vs. pallet (wooden platform); "palato" landed as gelato-adjacent

## Small / anecdotal observations (worth keeping)

- **"Like having your colors done."** Joy's own framing for the whole product — the app tells you what suits your palate the way a color analyst tells you which yellows suit you, so you stop wasting money on beans that are "so far off your palate." She returned to this analogy twice, unprompted, and clearly delighted in it. Strong candidate positioning line for a non-technical, older enthusiast.
- **The two coffees she still grieves:** Big Shoulders' Ugandan Bugisu (a roaster's own suggested substitute, the Burundi, "was never the same") and Onyx's Chalbesa Anaerobic (a short seasonal window). Both are re-buy-driven memories the log is built to hold — and both are *unre-acquirable*, which complicates the "buy again" value prop for seasonal coffee (echoes Jono's re-acquirability point from a different angle: here she'd want a *similar* coffee, not the same one).
- **Wine-vs-coffee seasonality thread:** she pushed back on the wine analogy herself — wine "lives on a shelf longer" once bottled, so it has "a little more predictability than the seasonality of certain roasts." A thoughtful, unprompted critique of the "Vivino for coffee" frame from the demand side.
- **The "pour more in" drinking style:** she refills her cup as it gets low ("I don't have a pot to draw from here… this is a one-shot deal" was the *café* case) — so at home she never experiences a single cup cooling and changing. Relevant to any "taste it hot, taste it cool" journaling feature: her habit erases the very variance such a feature would surface.
- **Numbing coffee at Trex:** bought a coffee that made her tongue numb "a couple times before I figured out what was going on" — a literal, physical version of the repeat-bad-purchase pain the log prevents.
- **Café-logging ask (unprompted, and specific):** she wants to log a coffee she loved *at a shop* — "that one cup of coffee may be more information for me than a bag of beans that I'm buying blindly." Facilitator's workaround (ask the barista, photograph the bag, pick the brew method) is a real path but not obvious in-product.
- **Pretentiousness allergy:** the Onyx barista who answered "should I wet the filter with warm or hot water?" with "you just have to try it and decide for yourself" read as condescending — "just tell me the answer." Her ask of Learn is the *opposite*: shoot straight, with a why. Ties directly to the expert-brew-recommendation request.
- **Expertise-as-trust shorthand:** on why an expert brew rec would land — "if somebody's an oncologist, you know they're going to work with cancer… you've got a title behind your name." She'll trust a named expert's ratio without needing to know who they are.
- **Grinder gate on spend:** she is *actively suppressing* her coffee spend until her grinder is repaired — "I think I'm getting subpar out of each coffee bean right now." A willingness-to-pay signal that's currently blocked by hardware, not interest.
- **Flavor-notes overwhelm, self-diagnosed:** faced with the descriptor list she said "this feels kind of overwhelming… I'm tasting all these things" and preferred to type ("cherry" → matched "red cherry"). She'd rather the list stay collapsed and type what she tastes — a clean vote for search-first over scroll (matches the descriptor UX Round 1 gestured at).
- **"Cheating" worry:** she asked whether reading the bag's notes before rating is cheating ("Oh, it says cherry, oolong, and pear"). A useful tell that users treat the rating as a self-test — the app should explicitly bless "taste is subjective, you don't have to match the bag."
- **Home-screen install landed:** facilitator added it to her home screen; she was happy to keep it as an app and re-log in.
- **Would evangelize:** Jay Lee, her pastor, travels the world with a camera-case kit of coffee gear — her first pick to tell. (A second coffee-obsessed churchgoer is too shy to approach.)
- **Name discussion, in full:** gelato association; the palate / palette / pallet spelling untangle; "if you had two T's in there, I might say pa-*lah*-to" (Italian phonetics). She'd say "palato" *because* it's gelato-adjacent — useful pronunciation signal (aligns with Jeremy).
